Say What, Satchel?

The Wisdom of Satchel Paige

Leroy Robert “Satchel” Paige (1906-1982), was a right-handed pitcher in both the Negro and Major Leagues and is considered by experts to be among the top twenty ballplayers of the 20th century. With a career spanning five decades, Satchel humbled the hitters of his day with arepertoire of colorfully-named pitches and uncanny accuracy— he could throw strikes over matchbooks.

Satchel was signed by the Cleveland Indins as a 42-year-old “rookie” midway through the 1948 season and was instrumental in the team winning the World Series that year. Players inclusive of Joe DiMaggio, Bob Feller and Josh Gibson agreed that Satchel was “the best pitcher they ever saw”.
